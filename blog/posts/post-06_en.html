3-Day Osaka Itinerary
Based at Oideya
2026 · Complete Day-by-Day Guide

📅 April 1, 2026✍️ Oideya Guest House

The ideal Osaka base gives you fast access to the main sights without stranding you in the tourist zone every evening. Oideya Guest House sits near Kanzakigawa Station — 6–7 minutes by train to Umeda — with a local seafood izakaya two minutes away on foot, a Michelin-listed udon shop one minute from the station, and the birthplace of negiyaki one stop up the line. This itinerary covers all of central Osaka across three days with no repeated destinations.

Day
1

Arrival: settle in, walk the neighbourhood, eat local

Check-in from 3pm. No need to go far — there's good food within two minutes.
From 3pm
Check in at OideyaBase
Check-in is from 3pm. If you arrive earlier, store luggage at a coin locker near the station and use the morning to explore Umeda or the surrounding area. From 3pm: collect your keys, look up at the dark timber beams in the living room, choose a bedroom, and put the kettle on.
Late afternoon
Walk Mitsutsuya ShotengaiLocal
Right outside Oideya's door: Mitsutsuya Shotengai, a covered shopping arcade roughly 550m long that has barely changed since its busiest decades. Walk it in both directions. The gap between this and Dotonbori — which you'll see tomorrow — is the whole point of staying here.
Evening
Dinner at Shunkashuutou seafood izakayaLocalFood
Two minutes on foot from Oideya: Shunkashuutou izakaya, where the owner buys the fish himself at market every morning. Fresh, seasonal, inexpensive. First night in a new city — the best possible use of not going anywhere. Sleep well; tomorrow is a full day.
2 min walk from Oideya (closed Mondays)

Day
2

Minami (Dotonbori, Namba, Shinsaibashi) + Juso

Osaka's most famous face by day. Stop in Juso for negiyaki on the way home.
Morning–Afternoon
Dotonbori & NambaSightseeing
Hankyu Kobe Line to Umeda (6–7 min), then Osaka Metro Midosuji Line to Namba (10 min). The Glico sign, Ebisu Bridge, the canal — walk both banks. Takoyaki from a street stall. Browse Kuromon Market. Lunch here: okonomiyaki, kushikatsu, or whatever you've been looking forward to. The density of restaurants is extraordinary.
Kanzakigawa → Umeda (Hankyu ~6–7 min) → Namba (Midosuji ~10 min) / ~25–35 min total
Afternoon
Shinsaibashi & Amerika-muraSightseeing
Walk north to Shinsaibashi-suji shopping arcade, then west to Amerika-mura — street art, vintage shops, fashion boutiques, cafes. This is Osaka's youth culture district; it's worth walking even if you don't buy anything. Take the afternoon slowly.
Evening
Juso: dinner at Negiyaki YamamotoLocalFood
On the way back, get off one stop early at Juso. Negiyaki Yamamoto (founded 1965, about 3 minutes from Juso Station's west exit) invented negiyaki — green onion, beef tendon, konnyaku, cooked flat on an iron griddle, dressed with soy and lemon. Queue if needed. This is the meal that closes a Dotonbori day well.
Get off at Juso on the way back (1 stop before Kanzakigawa) / ~3 min walk to restaurant

Day
3

Osaka Castle, Tennoji, Shinsekai + Umeda

History, a view from 300m, retro neon, and kushikatsu. End at Umeda for the journey home.
Morning–Lunchtime
Osaka Castle & Osaka Castle ParkSightseeing
Hankyu to Umeda, then JR Osaka Loop Line to Osaka-jokoen Station (about 15 min from Umeda). Allow a couple of hours: the castle keep houses a well-presented history museum, the top floor has a 360° city view, and the surrounding park is one of Osaka's most pleasant places to walk. Lunch at a park cafe or in the surrounding area before moving on.
Kanzakigawa → Umeda (Hankyu ~6–7 min) → Osaka-jokoen (JR Loop ~15 min) / ~30–40 min total
Afternoon
Tennoji & Abeno HarukasSightseeing
JR Loop Line from Osaka-jokoen to Tennoji (about 15 min). Abeno Harukas — 300m, an open-air observation floor — gives a clear-day view that reaches the mountains. Tennoji Zoo, Tennoji Park, and Shitenno-ji temple are all within walking distance if you want more.
Osaka-jokoen → Tennoji (JR Loop ~15 min)
Early evening
Shinsekai & kushikatsuSightseeingFood
About 10 minutes' walk from Tennoji: Shinsekai, a retro-neon neighbourhood centred on Tsutenkaku tower. The streets are dense with kushikatsu restaurants — deep-fried skewers with a communal sauce (no double-dipping, always). Order widely, drink cold beer, and let the neon do its work.
~10–15 min walk from Tennoji
Evening
Umeda: last shopping & journey homeSightseeing
From Shinsekai, head north to Umeda via Namba. Grand Front Osaka and Hankyu department store for final souvenirs. Then the Hankyu Kobe Line back to Kanzakigawa. If the schedule allows, save the final morning for Byakuan udon (opens 11:00, one minute from the station) before checking out.
Shinsekai → Namba → Umeda → Kanzakigawa (Hankyu ~6–7 min)

📋 Why this itinerary works

Each day covers a different part of Osaka with no overlap: local arrival on Day 1, Minami on Day 2, east Osaka and history on Day 3. Each evening brings something different — local seafood, negiyaki in Juso, kushikatsu in Shinsekai. The structure only makes sense because of where Oideya is: close enough to everything that travel is never a burden, local enough that the evenings feel genuinely different from the sightseeing.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does it take to get from Oideya to Dotonbori?
From Kanzakigawa Station, take the Hankyu Kobe Line to Osaka-Umeda (about 6–7 minutes), then the Osaka Metro Midosuji Line to Namba (about 10 minutes). Total travel time including the transfer walk is approximately 25–35 minutes.
What is the check-in time, and what should I do if I arrive early?
Check-in is from 3pm. If you arrive in the morning, use a coin locker at Kanzakigawa Station or Umeda Station, explore the city, and return for check-in from 3pm. Umeda is only 6–7 minutes away, making it easy to drop bags and head back out.
Is Kanzakigawa a convenient base for all of Osaka's main sights?
Yes. Umeda — Osaka's main transport hub — is 6–7 minutes away by train. From Umeda: Namba in 10 minutes (Midosuji Line), Osaka Castle in about 15 minutes (JR Loop), Tennoji in about 20–25 minutes. All major areas of central Osaka are within 30–40 minutes of Oideya.
Can I visit Kyoto or Kobe as day trips?
Easily. From Osaka-Umeda (6–7 min from Kanzakigawa), Kyoto-Kawaramachi is about 45 minutes on the Hankyu Kyoto Line, and Kobe-Sannomiya is about 30 minutes on the Hankyu Kobe Line.
